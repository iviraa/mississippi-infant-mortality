# Data dictionary

This document lists every dataset that goes into the project, where it comes from, and which columns we use.

## Sources

The seven data sources are downloaded by one Python script each in `pipeline/extract_*.py`. The table below lists what each script writes to disk and where it fetches the data from.

| Source | What lands on disk | Downloaded from |
|---|---|---|
| CDC PLACES (census tract, 2022 through 2025 release years) | `places_tract_2022.csv` through `places_tract_2025.csv`, one file per release year | The CDC Socrata API at `https://data.cdc.gov/resource/{dataset_id}.json`, filtered to `stateabbr=MS` |
| CDC/ATSDR Social Vulnerability Index 2022 | `svi_2022_ms_tract.csv` and `svi_2022_ms_county.csv` | `https://svi.cdc.gov/Documents/Data/2022/csv/states/Mississippi.csv` |
| US Census American Community Survey 5 year, 2023 vintage | `acs5_2023_ms_tract_long.csv`, a single long file with every variable from every table we use | The Census Bureau JSON API at `https://api.census.gov/data/2023/acs/acs5`, called once per table with `&for=tract:*&in=state:28` |
| CMS Provider Data Catalog | `hospital_general.csv`, `hcahps.csv`, `hrrp_fy2026.csv`, `complications_deaths.csv`, `timely_care.csv` | `https://data.cms.gov/provider-data/` |
| HRSA Health Professional Shortage Areas | `hpsa_primary_care.csv`, `hpsa_dental.csv`, `hpsa_mental_health.csv` | `https://data.hrsa.gov/DataDownload/DD_Files/` |
| TIGER/Line shapefiles, 2023 vintage | `tract.zip`, `county.zip`, `place.zip` | `https://www2.census.gov/geo/tiger/TIGER2023/` |
| Mississippi State Department of Health infant mortality | `data/seed/msdh_imr.csv` containing county and race specific infant mortality figures | Transcribed by hand from the MSDH 2025 Infant Mortality Report (Table 1 for state by year, Table 2 for race specific, Figure 7 for county 5 year aggregate) |

## Geography dimension

`dim_geography` has one row per Mississippi census tract. Census tracts are small statistical areas of roughly four thousand people each, and there are 875 of them across the state's 82 counties.

The surrogate primary key `geo_sk` is generated by Postgres and is referenced by every fact table that needs to point at a tract. The natural identifier is `tract_fips`, the eleven character GEOID assigned by the Census Bureau. It is stored as a string so that the leading zero on counties like Bolivar (`28011`) is preserved. The first five characters of `tract_fips` are copied into `county_fips` for convenience when grouping by county.

The `region` column is one of `Delta`, `Gulf Coast`, `Jackson Metro`, `Pine Belt`, or `Other`. The Delta tag covers the 18 counties of the official Mississippi Delta National Heritage Area. Two boolean shortcuts, `is_delta` and `is_gulf_coast`, are derived from `region` for the Delta versus rest comparisons that appear in many marts.

The two PostGIS columns are `geom` and `centroid_geom`. The first holds the full tract polygon as a `MultiPolygon` in NAD83 (EPSG 4269), which is what Folium reads when drawing choropleth maps. The second is the geographic center of the polygon, computed once at load time by `ST_Centroid(geom)`. Every later spatial query measures distance from the centroid.

## PLACES measures used

CDC PLACES publishes around 40 health measures at the census tract level and refreshes them annually. We ingest the eleven that map most directly to maternal and infant health risk factors plus the broader chronic disease burden.

| measure_id | What it measures | Higher value means |
|---|---|---|
| `DIABETES` | Adults with diagnosed diabetes | worse health |
| `BPHIGH` | Adults with high blood pressure | worse health |
| `OBESITY` | Adults with body mass index of 30 or higher | worse health |
| `CSMOKING` | Adults who currently smoke cigarettes | worse health |
| `DEPRESSION` | Adults with a diagnosis of depression | worse health |
| `MHLTH` | Adults reporting mental health was not good for 14 or more days in the past month | worse health |
| `ACCESS2` | Adults aged 18 to 64 without health insurance | worse health |
| `CHECKUP` | Adults who had a routine doctor visit in the past year | better health |
| `LACKTRPT` | Adults who lacked reliable transportation in the past year | worse health |
| `FOODINSECU` | Adults experiencing food insecurity in the past year | worse health |
| `HOUSINSECU` | Adults experiencing housing insecurity in the past year | worse health |

The PLACES values are model based small area estimates produced from the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System telephone survey, not direct observations. Every PLACES row ships with `low_confidence_limit` and `high_confidence_limit` columns that we preserve in `fact_places`.

## SVI variables used

The CDC and ATSDR Social Vulnerability Index summarizes 16 American Community Survey variables into four thematic scores plus an overall percentile rank.

Theme 1 covers socioeconomic status, using below 150 percent of poverty rate, unemployment rate, housing cost burden, percent of adults age 25 and older without a high school diploma, and uninsured rate. Theme 2 covers household characteristics, using percent age 65 or older, percent age 17 or younger, percent with a disability, percent in single parent households, and percent with limited English proficiency. Theme 3 captures racial and ethnic minority status as the percent of the population identifying as a racial or ethnic minority. Theme 4 covers housing type and transportation, using percent in multi unit housing, percent in mobile homes, percent in crowded housing, percent with no vehicle, and percent in group quarters.

Each per variable column starts with `EP_` for the estimate and is stored as a percentage. The theme scores are named `RPL_THEME1` through `RPL_THEME4` and are percentile ranks from 0 to 1, where 1 means the tract is the most vulnerable in the country on that theme. `RPL_THEMES` (plural) is the overall vulnerability percentile that combines all four themes.

The single most important data cleaning rule in this project is that the SVI uses the value `999` (stored as a negative number) to mean that no data is available. Every numeric SVI column passes through the `_svi_to_numeric` helper in `pipeline/load.py`, which converts the sentinel to a SQL `NULL` before the value is stored. Failing to do this would silently poison every aggregation that touched an SVI column.

## How everything joins

The pipeline relies on three identifier conventions so that facts from very different sources join cleanly.

Census tract joins use the eleven character `tract_fips` (also called GEOID), which appears in CDC PLACES, CDC SVI, ACS, and TIGER. This is the smallest geographic unit in the project. County joins use the five character `county_fips`, which is either stored directly (as in `fact_imr` and `fact_hpsa_county`) or derived as `LEFT(tract_fips, 5)`. Hospital joins use the CMS Certification Number (CCN), a six character identifier assigned by Medicare to every certified facility, which appears in every CMS published hospital file.

## Notes specific to each mart

### Maternal Risk Index

The maternal risk index is built as the simple mean of four normalized components: the average prevalence of pre existing chronic conditions (diabetes, high blood pressure, obesity, smoking), the average mental health distress (depression, frequent mental distress), the access risk (uninsured rate, lack of transportation), and the structural vulnerability (the SVI overall percentile scaled to 0 to 100). Each component is min max scaled within Mississippi before being averaged, which means the resulting score is interpretable as how this tract ranks within the state on this composite axis.

### Drive time estimate

The drive time mart computes the great circle distance from each tract centroid to the nearest birthing friendly hospital, then divides by 45 miles per hour to estimate the travel time. The 45 mile per hour figure is the standard approximation in the rural health literature for a mix of highway and back road driving. Future refinement could substitute a routing engine such as OSRM or the Google Maps API for actual road network drive times. The 30 minute threshold for declaring a care desert is empirically justified by the bimodal shape of the distance distribution, where most tracts cluster well below 30 minutes and the right tail of clearly underserved tracts begins past that mark.

### MSDH infant mortality

Single year county level infant mortality rates are suppressed by CDC WONDER for almost every Mississippi county because the deaths are rare enough that publishing them would identify individual families. The seed CSV therefore relies on three different aggregations from the MSDH 2025 Infant Mortality Report. State by year rates for 2020 through 2024 come from Table 1. Race specific 2024 rates (Black 15.2, White 5.8, Hispanic 6.3) come from Table 2. County level rates come from Figure 7, which reports a 5 year aggregate covering 2020 through 2024; counties with fewer than five infant deaths over that 5 year window are suppressed in the source and appear as `NULL` in our `imr_per_1000` column.

The 2024 state rate of 9.7 deaths per 1,000 live births, which is the highest in the nation, is what triggered the August 21, 2025 Public Health Emergency declaration that motivates this entire project.
